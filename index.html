Dear Daddy, This Father's Day means something different this year. Between last year and this one, I went through my first year of college, and if anything tests a bond, it's distance. I'm glad to say we didn't just survive it. We came out closer. I love this day because it's just ours, a daddy-daughter celebration. Not like a birthday, where everyone who's ever met you lines up to tell you how much you matter. Today, that's just me and Shivi's job. Do you remember when you, Mommy, and Shivi had to finally leave me at Georgia Tech? We were in the elevator going down toward the Uber that would take you all to the airport. Earlier, your flight had been delayed, and I was so happy, because it hadn't felt real yet that I was on my own in this unknown state, unknown city, unknown school. When the time came for all of you to leave, we sat outside Armstrong Hall, and Mommy and I cried. Maybe you also shed a tear or two. But what stood out to me most was the pillar you had become for us. Even if you felt sad, you didn't let it overtake you because you still had to do the hardest part and physically leave. This year, just listing things I like about you feels shallow. So here's the one I've admired the longest, the one nobody else in this family has, which is exactly why we all lean on you for it: Steadiness. Steadiness for others, even when you don't feel that steady yourself. I know the job search has been weighing on you. But you still show up for us like nothing's changed, which just proves my point. A job was never what made you who you are to this family. Your willingness to take us everywhere we've desired, to say yes to whatever we were chasing before even asking why, to put your own plans last, that's the real answer to who you are. So if there's a day you don't feel so steady, let us be the pillar for you. The way you were for me that day outside Armstrong Hall. Someone very wise once told me to leave uncertainties in God's hands. Take your own advice. Happy Father's Day. Infinite Love Always, Aishi ❤︎
